       We wanted to obtain two outputs in this experiment. One is released from step1 and the other is released from step2. The simplified reaction process is shown in Figure 4.

Methods

Step 1: We generated double-strand DNA. Each DNA was annealed by using thermal cycler (from 95 to 20℃, -1℃/min). The detail of annealing is shown below.
Table 2: The detail of annealing (* means complementary strand)

Step 2: First, we put transducerA, transducerB, gateA, gateB, fuelA, fuelB and trigger in tube. After that, we put input in tube. When we used inputAB as input, the outputA corresponds to output1 in Figure 4. And, when we used inputBA as input, the outputA corresponds to output2 in Figure 4. In this time, we divided experiment into two patterns (pattern1 and pattern2), and measured fluorescence intensity by using spectrofluorometer(FP6200, JASCO) to confirm whether the outputA is released. The outputA includes fluorescent molecule (6-FAM).
If you want to see detail of reaction process and role of fluorescence molecule, please see the web site of Team Sendai in biomod2014. The URL is shown below.

Pattern 2: We used inputBA
      We put above DNA and measured fluorescence intensity of outputA by using spectrofluorometer(FP6200,JASCO).
Figure 6 shows the fluorescence intensity of outputA at wavelength 518nm. We could confirm that outputA is released in Figure6. Therefore we could see that step2’s reaction of Figure 4 is achieved.

      In conclusion, we could confirm that fluorescence intensity of pattern2 is higher than pattern. This is because we set that pattern1’s outputA concentration is 80nM and pattern2’s outputA is 120nM.
Moreover, we could confirm that pattern2 needs reaction time more than pattern1. This is because in pattern2, outputA in step2 is released after outputB in step1 is released. For this reason, we could generate two outputs having different concentration by using Universal Strand Generator.

Step 3: Annealing
      Threshold and Gate:output were formed by cooling with incubator. (from 95 to 20℃, -2℃/min)
Step 4: Making 5% agarose gel
      We added agarose(5mg) and TBE buffer(95ml) and heated them in the microwave oven until agarose and TBE buffer completely dissolved. After the agarose had cooled, poured the agarose in the holder which was set the comb.Agarose hardened completely, removed the comb and moved agarose to chamber. We added TBE buffer to the chamber.

Table 9: TBE buffer Composition
Step 5: Toehold exchange reaction progression
      We put all DNA into the tube and mixed them at normal temperature.
Step 6: Running the agarose gel electrophoresis
      We added mixed DNA (5μl) and 6× Low Molecular Weight DNA Ladder(1μl). Next, we applied mixed solution (6μl) to each gel-well, and ran electrophoretic device at 100V, for 30mins.
When we finished electrophoresis, we moved gel to the container that is added GelRed (1μl). We shook the container with seesaw shaker. Putting elements in each lane as follow, and we verified output’s difference whether the input was high or low.
・Threshold gate
     1Lane Input(high),Threshold
     2Lane Input(low), Threshold
     3Lane Input(high), Threshold, Gate:output, Fuel
     4Lane Input(low), Threshold, Gate:output, Fuel
・NOT gate
     5Lane Input(high)
     6Lane Input(low)
     7Lane Input(high), Inverter, Threshold, Gate:output, Fuel
     8Lane Input(low), Inverter, Threshold, Gate:output, Fuel
